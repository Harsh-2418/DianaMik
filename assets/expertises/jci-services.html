About JCI

The Joint Commission International (JCI) is a globally recognized organization that sets international standards of quality and patient safety for Hospitals & healthcare organizations. JCI is the international division of The Joint Commission (USA), and its accreditation is considered the gold standard for hospitals worldwide.

In India, JCI accreditation complements NABH accreditation, as both are based on similar principles of quality, safety, and continuous improvement, under the framework of the Quality Council of India (QCI).

What is JCI Accreditation?

JCI Accreditation is a comprehensive evaluation process that assesses whether a healthcare organization meets international Hospital quality and safety standards for healthcare. It focuses on improving patient care outcomes, minimizing risks, and ensuring ethical and effective healthcare delivery.

A JCI-accredited hospital demonstrates its commitment to world-wide excellence in patient care and organizational management.

Objectives of JCI Accreditation

Continuous Improvement

To establish a culture of continuous quality improvement.

Patient-Centered Care

To ensure safe, effective, and patient-centered care.

Best Practices

To align hospital operations with international best practices.

Governance

To strengthen governance, leadership, and accountability.

Risk Management

To promote clinical excellence and risk management.

JCI Standards Framework

JCI standards are built around two major sections:

Patient-Centered Standards

  • International Patient Safety Goals (IPSG)
  • Access to Care & Continuity of Care (ACC)
  • Patient & Family Rights (PFR)
  • Assessment of Patients (AOP)
  • Care of Patients (COP)
  • Anesthesia & Surgical Care (ASC)
  • Medication Management & Use (MMU)

Healthcare Organization Management

  • Governance, Leadership & Direction (GLD)
  • Facility Management & Safety (FMS)
  • Staff Qualifications & Education (SQE)
  • Management of Information (MOI)
  • Quality Improvement & Patient Safety (QPS)
  • Prevention & Control of Infections (PCI)
  • Education & Research (optional)

Accreditation Process

01

Gap Analysis

Evaluate current systems and compliance with JCI standards.

02

Documentation

Preparation of manuals, SOPs, and operational guidelines.

03

Implementation

Orientation and process execution for all hospital departments.

04

Mock Surveys

Identify gaps and corrective actions before JCI’s on-site survey.

05

Official Survey

Comprehensive on-site assessment by JCI surveyors.

06

Award

Valid for 3 years with annual follow-up reviews.
