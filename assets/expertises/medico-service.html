About Medico-Legal Consulting

In today’s complex healthcare environment, medico-legal awareness and compliance are critical for protecting patients, healthcare professionals, and institutions.

Medico-Legal Consulting provides guidance and structured systems to ensure that hospitals, clinics, and healthcare providers operate in line with legal, ethical, and NABH-accreditation standards. At DianaMik Hospital Management Consultancy LLP, our team bridges the gap between medical practice and law, handling MLCs and grievances with accuracy and legal safety.

Objectives

To support healthcare organizations in:

  • Implementing legally compliant policies and procedures.
  • Managing medico-legal cases (MLCs) effectively.
  • Ensuring accurate documentation, consent, and reporting.
  • Preventing and mitigating medico-legal risks.
  • Enhancing staff awareness and NABH compliance in legal processes.

Scope of Medico-Legal Services

Our consultancy covers a wide range of hospital operations and clinical processes:

MLC Handling

Identification, registration, police coordination, and secure preservation of evidence for medico-legal cases.

Documentation & Consent

Preparation of informed consent forms, medical records, discharge summaries, and legally sound SOPs.

NABH & Compliance

Alignment with NABH standards (COP, PRE, IMS) and periodic legal documentation audits.

Risk Management

Investigation of incidents, root-cause analysis, and preventive measures for reducing legal exposure.

Liaison Support

Support in documentation for courts/tribunals and liaison with insurance, police, and government authorities.

NABH Alignment and Legal Relevance

By aligning with NABH chapters, hospitals ensure legal compliance and institutional protection.

NABH Chapter Key Focus Legal / Ethical Implication
PRE (Patient Rights) Informed consent & rights Legally valid documentation of consent.
COP (Care of Patients) Clinical care & MLC ID MLC reporting and handling.
IMS (Info Management) Confidentiality & Records Secure storage & legal access to records.
FMS (Facility Safety) Safety & Security Reporting of injury or assault cases.
CQI (Quality Improvement) Monitoring incidents Corrective and preventive legal action.
