About NABL

The National Accreditation Board for Testing and Calibration Laboratories (NABL) is a constituent board of the Quality Council of India (QCI). It provides third-party assessment and accreditation to laboratories conducting tests and calibrations, ensuring their technical competence and adherence to international standards.

Accreditation is based on globally accepted standards like ISO 15189, ISO/IEC 17025, and ISO 17043, recognized under ILAC and APLAC arrangements.

What is NABL Accreditation?

It is a formal recognition that a laboratory meets the required standards of quality, accuracy, and reliability. It assures patients and clinicians that results are scientifically valid, traceable, and globally accepted.

NABL-accredited labs are essential for ensuring quality in hospital diagnostic services, supporting evidence-based clinical decisions and patient care outcomes.

Objectives of NABL Accreditation

Quality Assurance

To promote quality assurance and standardized testing practices in Pathology Laboratory & healthcare.

Technical Competence

To ensure the technical competence of laboratories and staff.

Traceability

To maintain traceability of measurements and consistency of test results.

Confidence

To foster confidence among clinicians and patients in Pathology laboratory reports.

NABH Support

To support NABH-accredited hospitals in complying with diagnostic quality requirements.

Types of NABL Accreditations

Category Applicable Standard Purpose
Medical Testing Laboratories ISO 15189:2022 Diagnostic accuracy and patient result validity.
Testing Laboratories ISO/IEC 17025:2017 Quality assurance for materials, biological & chemical testing.
Calibration Laboratories ISO/IEC 17025:2017 Accuracy and traceability of measurement instruments.
Proficiency Testing Providers ISO/IEC 17043:2023 Ensures inter-laboratory result consistency.

Accreditation Process

01

Application

Submission of application and quality manual to NABL.

02

Pre-assessment

Preliminary review of readiness and system compliance.

03

On-site Audit

Detailed evaluation by NABL assessors for technical competence.

04

Correction

Addressing non-conformities identified during audit.

05

Grant

Accreditation certificate issued with validity for 2 years.

06

Surveillance

Periodic review to ensure continuous compliance.
