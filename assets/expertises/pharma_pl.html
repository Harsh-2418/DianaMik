About Pharmacy P&L Audit and Vigilance

The Pharmacy Profit & Loss (P&L) Audit and Vigilance process plays a critical role in maintaining the financial integrity, regulatory compliance, and operational efficiency of hospital pharmacies.

Our audit and vigilance framework is designed to help hospitals align their pharmacy operations with NABH, NABL, and regulatory standards, while also improving profitability and accountability.

Objectives of the Audit

  • To ensure accuracy and transparency in pharmacy revenue and expenditure.
  • To monitor and control procurement, storage, and dispensing as per legal norms.
  • To evaluate inventory management and wastage control.
  • To identify revenue leakages and compliance gaps.
  • To strengthen financial discipline and documentation aligned with NABH and Drug Control norms.

Scope of the Audit & Vigilance

Our program covers all key operational, financial, and compliance areas:

Financial Audit (P&L)

Includes sales/purchase reconciliation, margin analysis, credit management, and identification of profit leakages.

Operational Audit

Evaluates drug procurement, invoice verification, dispensing practices, and control over high-risk medications.

Compliance & Vigilance

Verifies licenses, NABH alignment, narcotic tracking, and adherence to the Drugs & Cosmetics Act.

Process & Quality Review

Covers prescription validation, LASA drug monitoring, temperature checks, and staff training compliance.
